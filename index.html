MST 1 syllabus

MEB, Fluid Flow, MO, CT (IOC), Statistics, HRM, & Startup Funding

MEB Syllabus

Unit I

  • Units and dimensions
  • Stoichiometric and composition relationships
  • Engineering calculations on process variables: flow rate, temperature, pressure
  • Ideal gas law calculations
  • Real gas relationships
  • Gaseous mixtures, vapor pressure and liquids
  • Saturation, partial saturation and humidity

Unit II

  • Material Balances on Non-reactive processes: Fundamental calculations, multiple-unit processes, recycle, by-pass and purge streams
  • Material Balances on Reactive processes: Molecular & Atomic Species Balance, extent of reaction method
  • Recycle and purge streams in reactive balances
  • Combustion calculations

Fluid Flow Syllabus

Unit I

  • Fluid Statics: Hydrostatic equilibrium, Manometers, Pressure measurements
  • Normal forces in fluids
  • Forces on submerged bodies
  • Buoyancy and stability

Unit II

  • Potential flow
  • Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids
  • Viscosity
  • Reynolds number
  • Nature of turbulence, Eddy viscosity
  • Flow in boundary layers (laminar & turbulent), transition length, boundary layer separation

Unit III

  • Bernoulli's equation
  • Kinetic energy & momentum correction factors
  • Pump work in Bernoulli's equation
  • Navier-Stokes equation

Size Reduction & Mechanical Separation

Unit I (CO1)

  • Crushers and Grinders: jaw crusher, crushing rolls, gyratory crusher
  • Tumbling/revolving mills, hammer mill, fluid energy mill
  • Closed and open circuit grinding
  • Power requirements, laws of crushing
  • Screening: stationary screens, grizzlies, trommel, vibrating screens
  • International & Indian Standard Screens
  • Screening analysis: differential and cumulative

Unit II (CO2)

  • Motion of particles through fluids: Stokes’ law, Newton’s law
  • Free and hindered settling
  • Setting tanks, double cone classifiers
  • Batch & continuous thickeners
  • Settling chamber, cyclone, filter bag, electrostatic precipitators
  • Centrifugation: tubular bowl centrifuge, disk centrifuge, batch basket centrifuge

CT (IOC) Syllabus

Unit I

  • Chlor-alkali industry: brine electrolysis, current & energy efficiency
  • Diaphragm, mercury and Dow cells
  • Caustic soda, chlorine
  • Soda Ash: Solvay and modified Solvay processes, handling & safety
  • Sulphuric Acid: Chamber & contact processes, materials of construction, storage & handling

Unit II

  • Cement: types, constituents, manufacture of Portland cement
  • Glass: introduction, types, raw materials, manufacture

Statistics Syllabus

Unit I

  • Definition & types of statistics
  • Types of variables
  • Organising data: histograms, stem-and-leaf, bar/pie charts, scatter plots
  • Data preparation: editing, coding, entry, cross-tabulation
  • Descriptive measures: frequency distributions, central tendency, variation
  • Skewness (meaning & coefficient), Kurtosis
  • Probability: definitions, rules, conditional probability, independence
  • Bayes’ theorem
  • Random variables and distributions: Binomial, Poisson, Uniform, Normal

Unit II

  • Sampling distributions: mean & proportion
  • Central limit theorem applications
  • Sampling techniques
  • Estimation: point & interval estimates (large & small samples)
  • Sample size determination & practical considerations

HRM Syllabus

Unit I

  • Meaning, scope, objectives, functions & importance of HRM
  • Evolution of HRM
  • Strategic role of HRM
  • HRM vs HRD (comparative analysis)
  • Environment of HRM: government, internal & external forces
  • HRM practices in India

Unit II

  • Human Resource Planning: definition, objectives, process & importance
  • Job analysis, description, specification & evaluation
  • Recruitment, selection, placement, induction
  • HRD: training & development
  • Career planning & development
  • Promotions, demotions, transfers, separations
  • Absenteeism & turnover

Startup & Funding Syllabus

Unit I

  • Financial statements: balance sheet, income statement
  • Cash flow statement
  • Financial ratios: profitability, efficiency, liquidity, leverage

Unit II

  • Financial planning: readiness to raise capital
  • Pro-forma financial statements
  • Cash flow planning & burn rate
